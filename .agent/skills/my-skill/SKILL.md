# my-skill

Team Management Skills All Professionals Need

## Instructions

1. Creativity skills
Successful team managers will develop and apply creativity skills and encourage creativity within their teams. A strong leader understands that forward momentum to achieve objectives can sometimes require innovative approaches to completing projects and that their team's creativity can be a powerful source for new ideas that influence positive changes and outcomes.

2. Communication skills
Strong communication skills are necessary for leading teams, and effective leaders rely on these skills every day to interact with their teams, colleagues and employers. Effective communication encompasses the ability to listen actively, ask questions for more information, convey information to your team and give and receive feedback. Additionally, nonverbal forms of communication like written communication and reading body language to understand how your listening and speaking skills affect others are essential in directing and managing a team of employees.

3. Organizational skills
The ability to organize basic work assignments, break down complex projects, and prioritize and delegate tasks are essential in a leadership role. Organizational skills and attention to detail allows managers to direct functions and processes and organize and maintain the assignments, workflow and schedules 
for their teams.
4. Time management skills
The ability to manage multiple employee schedules, set and achieve goals and work within multiple deadlines is crucial to managing a team. These time management skills allow managers and professionals in leadership roles to perform efficiently and stay on track with important projects. Additionally, understanding how to set deadlines according to priority is another time management skill that many successful leaders have.

5. Decision-making skills
Decision-making skills encompass a range of traits that leaders depend on to make choices between options and alternatives. The ability to think analytically and consider different perspectives and alternatives demonstrates a leader's commitment to thinking carefully and implementing decisions for the benefit of the company. Additionally, the ability to rely on both logical thinking and intuitive nuances when weighing benefits and drawbacks to various choices is something successful professionals demonstrate in leadership roles.

6. Strategic planning skills
Strategic planning involves focusing on implementing processes and strategies that achieve goals, improve operations and initiate beneficial changes. Strong team management requires vision, direction and knowing how to motivate and influence your team to contribute to the overall accomplishment of company objectives. Strategic planning also means understanding how to allocate resources, achieve results at reduced costs, support your team's unique skills and talent and provide the tools your team needs to succeed.

7. Problem-solving skills
Great leaders approach challenges and create solutions that move their organizations toward their goals. Effective problem-solving requires identifying challenges and finding ways to overcome them. This involves analyzing details, weighing alternative solutions and implementing strategies that help solve issues in the workplace. Additionally, evaluation and reflection on solutions you implement is another important aspect of your problem-solving skills.

8. Emotional intelligence
Emotional intelligence is highly important in the workplace, especially in a leadership role. Empathy for others' thoughts and feelings, compassion when engaging with others and the ability to seek understanding are aspects of your emotional intelligence that you'll need as a team manager. Along with these important traits, it's important to develop your abilities to be aware of and regulate your thoughts and emotions.

9. Interpersonal skills
Interpersonal skills encompass traits that leaders need to interact with their teams and superiors. Being able to consider everyone's feelings, input and feedback demonstrates a leader's effectiveness in directing and collaborating within their team. In addition to this, interpersonal skills include being able to take responsibility, hold yourself accountable and find ways to understand others.

10. Adaptability
Adaptable team managers must remain open to transition, be able to initiate change, have the ability to improve and demonstrate a desire to develop and learn. Capable leaders adapt when taking on diverse projects within diverse environments and find ways to approach differences in the workplace that lead to beneficial outcomes. Adaptable leaders are also flexible with new implementations and understand how to evaluate the effects of strategies and processes they direct.
